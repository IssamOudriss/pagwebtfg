About the Project

This project investigates how computer vision models can be efficiently deployed on resource-constrained edge devices, such as the Jetson Nano, with future potential for FPGA integration. With a focus hardware-aware optimization, the work blends practical AI deployment with telecommunications engineering applications.

Challenges include compatibility with 2025 software stacks, power and thermal limitations, and striking a balance between inference accuracy and model size. The study also explores future expansion possibilities in networked edge environments.

The main idea consists on bringing the new world of AI and neural networks to small low-level applications in telecom engineering fields such as Internet of Things, Smart Cities or even Domotics.

Other potential applications for this low-level technologies might be automathic inspection of buildings for structural damage or crop disease, secure logging and logging management, or even early wild fire detection and wild-life monitoring in remote areas.

Goals

  • Deploy and evaluate a computer vision model on Jetson Nano under modern constraints.
  • Deploying a variety of models on the Jetson Nano with a main focus on application limitations.
  • Optimize the model for real-time performance and low power consumption.
  • Analyze edge inference trade-offs vs. cloud-based processing.
  • Open-source optimized configurations and findings.
  • (Coming soon) Explore FPGA integration on other edge devices for performance comparisson.
