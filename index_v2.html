About the Project

Modern artificial intelligence is currently facing several significant challenges that hinder its widespread adoption. These include its high energy consumption, which requires substantial power resources, and its need for massive computational power, which often exceeds the capabilities of many devices. Furthermore, AI‚Äôs dependence on data centers means it relies heavily on cloud infrastructure, making it unsuitable for use in environments that require real-time decision-making or operate in remote, disconnected locations.

As a result, its application in areas such as autonomous vehicles, smart manufacturing, and high-performance robotics remains limited.

The Core of the Project

In this project, I am exploring the intersection of computer vision and edge computing, focusing on deploying AI models in resource-constrained environments. The goal is to bring AI to small, portable devices like the Jetson Nano, which can run complex algorithms efficiently while being lightweight and cost-effective.

The goal is to enable real-time image and video analysis in remote, autonomous, and low-cost systems, such as drones, robots, and security systems, without relying on cloud infrastructure.

Goals

  • Deploy and evaluate a computer vision model on Jetson Nano under modern constraints.
  • Deploying a variety of models on the Jetson Nano with a main focus on application limitations.
  • Optimize the model for real-time performance and low power consumption.
  • Analyze edge inference trade-offs vs. cloud-based processing.
  • Open-source optimized configurations and findings.
  • (Coming soon) Explore FPGA integration on other edge devices for performance comparisson.
